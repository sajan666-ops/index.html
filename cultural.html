CULTURAL HISTORY OF FARIDKOT

Culturally rich, Faridkot is home to the Faridkot Fort, built in the 18th century and expanded by Raja Wazir Singh. The town is deeply connected to Baba Farid (1173â€“1266), whose verses are included in the Guru Granth Sahib. Annual fairs (melas) like Baba Farid Agman Purb attract thousands. Traditional arts like Punjabi embroidery (phulkari), giddha, and bhangra are still practiced, and over 200 religious sites, including gurudwaras and temples, contribute to its spiritual fabric.
