ENVIROMENTAL HISTORY OF FARIDKOT

Faridkot spans 1,458 km², with 80% of land under cultivation. Initially a semi-arid zone, British-built canals enabled irrigation, later expanded with tube wells. Over time, groundwater levels have declined from 15m in 1990 to 35m in 2020. Excessive use of fertilizers and pesticides has led to rising cancer cases, prompting the launch of the “Cancer Train” from Bhatinda to Bikaner. The Punjab government has started promoting organic farming and crop diversification, though only 5–7% of land is currently under eco-friendly agriculture.
