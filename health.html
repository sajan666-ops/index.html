HEALTH IN FARIDKOT

Faridkot is a prominent healthcare hub in southern Punjab due to the presence of key medical institutions. The district has a mix of government and private healthcare facilities.

1. MAJOR HEALTHCARE INSTITUTIONS


Guru Gobind Singh Medical College and Hospital (GGSMCH)
Established: 1973
Capacity: ~1,200 beds
Location: Sadiq Road, Faridkot
Departments: General Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, Cardiology, Neurology, Dermatology, ENT, Orthopedics, Psychiatry, Radiology, ICU/CCU, etc.
Significance: One of Punjab's oldest and most reputed government medical colleges.
Facilities: Offers MBBS, MD, MS, nursing, and paramedical courses; has a 1,000+ bed hospital.
Specialties: Cardiology, Neurology, Pediatrics, Oncology, ENT, General Surgery, etc


Baba Farid University of Health Sciences (BFUHS)
Established: 1998
Role: Regulates medical education and affiliations for health sciences colleges across Punjab.
Departments:
Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry, Physiotherapy, Nursing, Pharmacy, and Public Health
Also manages exams (e.g., NEET PG/UG counseling in Punjab)
Includes: Medical, dental, physiotherapy, nursing, and pharmacy colleges.
Location: Near GGS Medical College


2. GOVERNMENT HEALTH FACILITIES

Civil Hospital, Faridkot: Offers general medical care, maternity services, and emergency response.
Community Health Centres (CHCs): At Kotkapura, Jaito, and other blocks.
Capacity: ~150 beds Primary Health Centres (PHCs): Over 25 PHCs across rural areas.
Sub-Centres: Health outposts in villages for immunization, first aid, maternal care.


3. HEALTH SERVICES & PROGRAMS

Ambulance: 108 emergency service
Vaccination Drives: COVID-19, polio, hepatitis, etc.
National Health Missions (NHM): Active in maternal-child health, family planning, and tuberculosis control.
Jan Aushadhi Kendras: Provide generic medicines at low cost.
Mobile Medical Units: Regularly visit remote villages.

4. PRIVATE HEALTHCARE SECTOR

Well-known Clinics/Hospitals:
Khera Hospital (Multi-specialty)
Saran Hospital
Brar Hospital
Nursing Homes: Spread across Faridkot and Kotkapura towns
Pharmacies: Generic & branded drug stores, Jan Aushadhi Kendras for affordable medicine

5. PUBLIC HEALTH PROGRAMS & SERVICES

Maternal & Child Health (MCH)
Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY): Cash benefits for institutional delivery
Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK): Free medicines, delivery, transport
Anganwadi Integration: Nutrition for children 0–6 years, pregnant/lactating women

Immunization
Mission Indradhanush: Focused on complete immunization of children
Regular vaccinations: BCG, DPT, Polio, Measles, Hepatitis B
Cold Chain Maintenance Units at block level
Screening & Awareness Campaigns

Non-Communicable Diseases (NCD) Cell: Diabetes, BP, cancer screening
School Health Programs: Eye, dental, anemia screening
Menstrual Hygiene Campaigns in girls’ schools
Disease Control Programs

RNTCP: Revised National TB Control Program (DOTS centers in each block)
NVBDCP: Vector-borne diseases (malaria, dengue)
HIV/AIDS Programs: Counseling and testing centers (ICTC), ART centre at GGSMCH

6. EMERGENCY AND MOBILE SERVICES

108 Ambulance Service: Government-funded emergency response vehicles
102 Janani Express: For pregnant mothers to reach health facilities
Mobile Medical Units (MMUs): Weekly visits to underserved villages
eSanjeevani Telemedicine: Free video consultations with doctors

KEY CHALLENGES IN HEALTH SECTOR in FARIDKOT

Doctor Shortages: Especially specialists at PHC/CHC level
Rural Access: Many remote villages still face travel issues for treatment
Referral Overload: GGSMCH overburdened as it serves multiple districts
Awareness Gap: Poor knowledge on preventive health, menstrual hygiene
Mental Health & Drug De-addiction: Need for more rehab centers



RECENT GOVERNMENT INITIATIVES

Ayushman Bharat PMJAY: Free healthcare up to ₹5 lakh for BPL families
Health ID Card (ABHA): For digital records & e-hospitals
Nasha Mukt Punjab Abhiyan: Drug abuse prevention programs & rehab support
Mission Sehat Punjab: Free diagnostics, OPD/IPD upgrades
Health & Wellness Centres (HWCs): Expanded PHCs with chronic disease care
