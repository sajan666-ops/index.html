ECONOMIC HISTORY OF FARIDKOT

Agriculture has been the backbone of Faridkot’s economy, with nearly 80% of its population engaged in farming or related activities. It produces over 450,000 tonnes of wheat and over 350,000 tonnes of rice annually. The Green Revolution of the 1960s brought tractors, tube wells, and high-yield seeds to the region. Cotton ginning and rice mills are prominent in the industrial belt of Kotkapura. The per capita income of Faridkot (2023 estimates) is ₹1.45 lakh per annum, supported significantly by foreign remittances from Canada, UK, and Australia.
