POLITICAL HISTORY OF FARIDKOT

Faridkot, founded in the 13th century by Raja Mokalsi, was named after Sufi saint Baba Farid. It emerged as a princely state covering 1,652 km² during the British era under the Cis-Sutlej Agency. The state had its own army, court, and revenue system. Faridkot’s loyalty to the British during the 1857 Revolt earned it favor, and its rulers retained partial sovereignty until merging with the Indian Union in 1948. It became a separate district in 1972, and has since played a key role in state politics, contributing MLAs and ministers to Punjab's government.
