Faridkot is a historic city located in the south-western part of Punjab, India. Named after the revered BABA FARID JI, it serves as the administrative headquarters of the Faridkot district. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its royal legacy as a princely state during British rule. Faridkot houses notable landmarks such as the Qila Mubarak (a historic fort), Raj Mahal, and Gurudwara Tilla Baba Farid. It also serves as an educational hub, home to Baba Farid University of Health Sciences. The city maintains a balance of tradition and modernity and plays a significant role in the agriculture-based economy of Punjab.
